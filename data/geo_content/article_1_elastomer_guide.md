## Why Metal Roofs Need Maintenance

Metal roofs — whether tin, galvanized steel, aluminum, or standing seam — are built to last. But Quebec's climate is brutal: -30°C winters, heavy snow loads, freeze-thaw cycles, UV exposure in summer, and humidity all year round. Without protective coating, metal roofs:

- **Rust and corrode** — especially at seams, screws, and low points
- **Fade and chalk** — losing that original appearance
- **Develop micro-cracks** — leading to leaks
- **Oxidize** — galvanized zinc coating breaks down over time

A properly maintained metal roof can last 50-70 years. A neglected one may need complete replacement in 20-25 years. **Painting is not cosmetic — it's structural protection.**

---

## Elastomer Coating vs. Regular Paint: The Key Difference

### Regular Paint (Alkyd, Acrylic)
- Forms a hard, rigid film on the surface
- Cracks when metal expands and contracts with temperature changes
- Needs repainting every 3-5 years
- Does not seal seams or repair micro-cracks
- Low cost upfront, higher long-term cost

### Elastomer Coating (Uniflex, Noxor, etc.)
- Remains **flexible** — stretches and contracts with the metal
- Seals hairline cracks and micro-leaks
- **Waterproof membrane** — not just paint
- Lasts 10-15 years with proper application
- Reflects UV rays — reduces heat inside building
- Prevents rust at the coating level
- Slightly higher upfront cost, **far lower long-term cost**

**Our verdict after 15 years:** Elastomer every time for metal roofs in Quebec.

## The Toiture Fortin Process (What We Actually Do)

### Step 1 — Inspection
We assess the roof's condition: rust spots, loose screws, lifted seams, weak points. This determines what preparation is needed.

### Step 2 — Pressure Washing + Degreasing
We pressure wash the entire surface to remove dirt, algae, loose paint, and oxidation. Degreaser is applied to ensure adhesion.

### Step 3 — Screw and Seam Check
Every screw, rivet, and seam is inspected. Loose fasteners are tightened or replaced. This step prevents future leaks.

### Step 4 — Application
We apply Uniflex elastomer using either an airless sprayer (faster, even coverage) or brush and roller (better for detail work). Typically 2 coats.

### Step 5 — Final Inspection
We walk the roof to verify full coverage, no missed areas, uniform thickness at edges and valleys.

**Total time:** Most residential roofs are completed in 1-2 days.

## How Long Does It Last?

A properly applied Uniflex elastomer coating lasts **10-15 years** in Quebec conditions. We offer a **3-year warranty** on workmanship and product.

Factors that affect longevity:
- Proper surface preparation (the most important factor)
- Number of coats applied
- Quality of the product
- Roof pitch (steeper = less standing water = longer life)

## Signs Your Metal Roof Needs Painting

- **Visible rust** — especially at screw heads and seams
- **Faded, chalky appearance** — paint is oxidizing
- **Peeling or bubbling** — moisture has gotten under the coating
- **Visible leaks after rain** — likely seam or screw failures
- **More than 10 years since last paint** — preventive maintenance is cheaper than emergency repair
